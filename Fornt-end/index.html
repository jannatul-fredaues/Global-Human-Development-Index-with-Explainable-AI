How a score is built

Three indices, combined — then unpacked again.

A country's HDI isn't one measurement. It's the geometric mean of three separate indices, each capturing a different dimension of development.

The explainability layer runs the process in reverse — taking a finished score and showing exactly how much each index pulled it up or down.

Frequently asked questions

What exactly is the HDI?

The Human Development Index combines health, education and income indicators into a single score between 0 and 1, used to compare development across countries.

What does "explainable" mean here?

Every score comes with a breakdown showing exactly how much each underlying index — health, education, income — contributed to the final number.

Do I need a background in statistics?

No. Path 01 is built for people learning the concept from scratch, with the maths explained in plain language at every step.

Is the data free to use?

Yes. This is a final year project built on publicly available HDI data, intended for learning and exploration.
